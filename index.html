Privacy Policy for G Flow Last Updated: 12/14/2023 1. Introduction Welcome to G Flow ("us," "we," or "our"). This Privacy Policy is designed to help you understand how G Flow accesses and displays Gmail messages and Google Calendar events within your browser. 2. Information Collection and Usage G Flow accesses your Gmail messages and Google Calendar events within the confines of your browser to provide a seamless popup interface for your convenience. The extension does not collect, store, or transmit any user data, including Gmail messages, Calendar events, or personal information, to external servers. 3. Chrome Authentication and API Access To enable G Flow to access Gmail and Google Calendar, the extension requires you to be logged into Chrome. G Flow utilizes your Chrome authentication token to access the Gmail and Google Calendar APIs. This token is solely used for the purpose of fetching your Gmail messages and Calendar events to display them within the extension. 4. Data Displayed to the User G Flow displays your Gmail messages, Google Calendar events, and your email address within the extension interface. It's important to note that this information is not sent or tracked outside of your local browser. G Flow operates solely within your browser, and all data remains on your computer. 5. Storage and Security G Flow may utilize Chrome storage for local storage purposes, enhancing your experience by remembering certain preferences or settings. This storage is entirely local and does not involve the transmission of data to external servers. We prioritize the security of your data and ensure that all operations are performed within the confines of your browser. 6. Changes to This Privacy Policy G Flow may update this Privacy Policy to reflect any changes in its practices. Any updates will be effective immediately upon posting. 7. Contact Us If you have any questions or concerns about our Privacy Policy or the operation of G Flow, please contact us at g.flow.ext@gmail.com.
